# Suggested Response Templates for Schilmoeller & Schoenfield, PC

These templates are based on a detailed analysis of 29 of Jane's actual client emails. Every phrase, greeting, sign-off, and structural pattern below was pulled directly from how Jane already communicates. The goal is to preserve Jane's warm, professional voice exactly as it is -- not to "improve" it -- so that automated drafts feel indistinguishable from Jane writing the email herself.

Staff reviewers should verify that placeholders (in [brackets]) are filled in correctly before approving any draft.

---

## How Jane Writes: Quick Reference

| Element | Jane's Pattern |
|---|---|
| **Greeting (clients)** | "Good morning," / "Hi [Name]," / "Hi [Name] -" |
| **Personal touch** | Always included for client-facing emails. Comments on travel, retirement, family, projects. |
| **Body structure** | Numbered lists for questions and action items. Bullet points (*) for sub-items. |
| **Staff delegation** | Names the staff member, explains what they will do, and asks client to cc them. |
| **Closing** | "Thanks and have a great day," / "Thanks and have a wonderful day," / "Thanks!" |
| **Signature** | "Jane" on its own line. Full signature block on first emails in a thread. |
| **Tax explanations** | Leads with the conclusion/benefit, then explains mechanics. Uses "Make sense?" after complex topics. |
| **Reassurance** | "Late K-1s are a common event, so we are on top of it." / "If limited, the excess carries over - so it is not lost." |

## Template 1: Tax Return Delivery Notification

**When to use:** A client's business or individual return has been completed and is ready for review on the portal.

**Template:**

> Hi [Client Name] -
>
> I hope things are going well with you these days!
>
> We have completed the following [Year] returns for [Entity Name]:
>
> * Federal Return ([Form Type]):     [No tax due / $X tax due]
> * [State] Return:           [No tax due / $X tax due]
>
> [Tomorrow / Later today], Sandra Rivera (cc'd above) will upload the returns to our portal for your review and approval. She will send you a separate email when it has been uploaded.
>
> [If applicable:]
> How is your personal tax information coming along? Please email Sandra when it is ready for pickup and we will schedule a courier.
>
> Please let me know if you have any questions.
>
> Thanks and have a great day!
>
> Jane

**Derived from:** Jane's emails to Shelley (Angel Island Properties), Jacqueline (Beyond the Blackboard), Curt (Outfitter Energy), and Grace (Regional Park). Every one of these follows the same pattern: personal touch, completed items as bullet list, portal upload timing with Sandra named, invitation for questions.

>    a. Documentation from your broker regarding the date, cost basis, and valuation on contribution date.
>    b. The standard contribution letter from your [church/charity].
>    c. [If applicable:] Depending on your planned charitable giving, it might make sense to group contributions into one year and then take advantage of the standard deduction in the next year. If helpful, we can help with planning when you are ready to make the contribution.

> Jane

## Notes for Sara and Jane

1. **These templates preserve Jane's actual voice.** Phrases like "so it is not lost," "Make sense?", "things are quite busy at the office these days," and "we are on top of it" are Jane's own words from real emails.

2. **Personal touches matter.** Jane almost always opens client-facing emails with a personal comment. The AI system should use any available context about the client (travel, retirement, family events mentioned in prior emails) to generate an appropriate personal touch.

3. **Staff delegation is a pattern, not an afterthought.** Jane consistently names the staff member, explains what they will do, and asks the client to cc them. This should be replicated in every response where staff are involved.

4. **Numbered lists are Jane's signature.** She uses them for questions, action items, and tax guidance. The AI should default to numbered lists when presenting multiple items.

5. **Jane closes with warmth.** "Thanks and have a great day!" is the most common closing. "Thanks and have a wonderful day," appears for more formal or detailed communications. Plain "Thanks!" is used for brief internal emails.
